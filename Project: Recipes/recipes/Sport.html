Drill Name

Full-Court Layup & Conditioning Drill

Description

A high-intensity basketball drill that combines layup practice with cardiovascular conditioning, helping players improve finishing at the rim while building endurance.

Description Text

This drill requires players to sprint the full court, execute layups on both ends, and repeatedly transition between offense and defense. It simulates game-speed fatigue, sharpening focus, footwork, and finishing under pressure. Ideal for guards and wings, but beneficial for all positions.

Equipment / Gear

  • Basketball
  • Full basketball court
  • Cones (optional for spacing)
  • Stopwatch or timer
  • Water bottles for hydration breaks

List of Equipment / Gear

  • Basketball – 1 (size 7 for men, size 6 for women)
  • Full basketball court – 1
  • Cones – 4 (optional)
  • Stopwatch or timer – 1
  • Water bottles – as needed

Steps (Unordered)

  • Start at the baseline with a basketball.
  • Sprint to the opposite end and make a right-handed layup.
  • Get your own rebound and sprint back to the starting end.
  • Make a left-handed layup on the return.
  • Repeat for 60 seconds without stopping.
  • Rest for 30 seconds.
  • Repeat the set 3–5 times.
  • Record makes and misses to track improvement.

Ordered Steps

  1. Start at the baseline with one basketball.
  2. Sprint to the opposite end of the court.
  3. Perform a right-handed layup.
  4. Get your own rebound immediately.
  5. Sprint back to the starting baseline.
  6. Perform a left-handed layup.
  7. Get the rebound again.
  8. Repeat steps 2–7 continuously for 60 seconds.
  9. Rest for 30 seconds.
  10. Complete 3 to 5 total sets.
  11. Count successful layups after each set.
  12. Cool down with light jogging and stretching.
